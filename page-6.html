Understanding the Root Cause

Hair thinning is not simply a cosmetic concern—it's a biological process rooted in cellular energy depletion. Hair follicles contain mitochondria, the powerhouses of cells, which require consistent energy to maintain the anagen (growth) phase of the hair cycle. As we age or encounter stress, hormonal changes, and environmental factors, mitochondrial function declines, shortening the growth phase and prolonging the resting phase, leading to progressively thinner hair.
